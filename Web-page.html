About Volleyball

  1. What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players are separated by a net. The objective is to send the ball over the net so that the opposing team cannot return it or prevent it from touching the ground in their court.

Brief History
  1. Invented: 1895 by William G. Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA.
  2. Originally called "Mintonette".
  3. Created as a less intense alternative to basketball.
  4. Became an official Olympic sport in 1964 (Tokyo Olympics).

Basic Rules

  • Teams: 6 players on each side (3 front, 3 back).
  • Scoring:
  • A point is scored on every rally (rally scoring).
  • A set is played to 25 points , must win by 2.
  • Matches are best of 5 sets (the final set is to 15).
  • Hits per Side: Each team can touch the ball a maximum of 3 times before sending it over the net.
  • Rotation: Players rotate clockwise each time they win the serve from the opponents.
